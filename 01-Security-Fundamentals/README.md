🔍 Key Terminology
To communicate like a professional, you need to understand the basic vocabulary of cyber security:

Vulnerability: A weakness in a system, application, or network. (Example: An outdated, unpatched Windows server).

Threat: A potential danger that could exploit a vulnerability to breach security. (Example: A hacker, malware, or even a natural disaster).

Risk: The potential for loss or damage when a threat exploits a vulnerability.

Formula: Risk = Threat × Vulnerability

Exploit: A piece of software or a sequence of commands that takes advantage of a vulnerability.
